Solve For The Shaded Area. “You should be able to solve this.”

Posted July 26, 2018 By Presh Talwalkar. Read about me, or email me.

I received this problem from Reio in Romania.

What is the area of the shaded region? The puzzle was distributed with the phrase “You should be able to solve this.”

Described verbally: Start with a point in the interior of a square. For each side of the square, draw a line segment between the midpoint of the side to the interior point. This divides the square into four regions with areas of 16, 20, 32, and x (areas listed clockwise from the southwest region). What is the value of x?

Answer To Solve For The Shaded Area

(Pretty much all posts are transcribed quickly after I make the videos for them–please let me know if there are any typos/errors and I will correct them, thanks).

Here is the key principle to solve this problem. Two triangles that have the same dimensions for their base and their height will have the same area.

So let’s create some triangles!

Connect the corners of the square to the point in the center, dividing the square into 8 triangles.

On each side of the square, the two triangles have the same dimensions for their base (half the square’s side), and the same dimensions for their height (distance to the point inside the square).

So let’s say we have triangles with areas a, b, c, d such that:

16 divided into a, b
20 divided into b, c
32 divided into c, d
x divided into d, a

From here we can deduce that the sum of opposite areas are equal:

(a + b) + (c + d) = (b + c) + (a + d)

We also know:

a + b = 16
b + c = 20
c + d = 32

So let’s substitute

(a + b) + (c + d) = (b + c) + (d + a)
16 + 32 = 20 + (d + a)
16 + 32 – 20 = d + a = x

And that’s the answer! The unknown area is equal to 28.
